Welcome to My Journey

Hello, my name is Deki Lhazom. I am from Narang village in Mongar District, born on April 28, 2006. Since beginning my educational journey, I've discovered the importance of self-growth, resilience, and contributing to the world around me. This website is a tapestry of my experiences, from sports and cultural activities to leadership roles and community projects. It's a journey of embracing my unique self while striving to make a positive impact. Thank you for visiting!

About My Journey

Early Life & Education

Born on April 28, 2006, in Narang village, Mongar District, I began my schooling at Narang Primary in 2013. Though I was often quiet, I always said yes to leadership roles and group activities, which helped me grow. The Bhutan Baccalaureate curriculum and the support of my teachers have been vital in shaping my path. Joining the Royal Academy in 2020 taught me that being educated is more than just gaining knowledge; it's about self-discovery and meaningful experiences. I've been on a continuous journey of self-growth, especially through the five areas of development, learning to be a person of substance.

My Passions

Running & Sports

From a young age, I've been passionate about sports and running. Each run, whether a cross-country event or a half marathon, has been a battle between my body and mind, teaching me discipline, patience, and resilience. As a member of my school's basketball and football teams, I learned the importance of teamwork, sportsmanship, and endurance. I realized that true success lies in finishing strong, breaking personal barriers, and supporting my teammates.

Bhutanese Culture & Traditions

I am deeply passionate about my Bhutanese culture and traditions. I have actively participated in indigenous games like Degor and performed traditional dances during special occasions. These experiences have not only strengthened my cultural pride and discipline but have also deepened my appreciation for the meaningful connections our traditions create. Representing my school in a traditional dance exchange program was an enriching experience that allowed me to share our heritage with others.
